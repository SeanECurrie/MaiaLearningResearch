## Executive Summary

### Purpose of Positioning Maps

Competitive positioning maps visualize how competitors are positioned relative to each other across strategic dimensions. These maps help identify:
- **Strategic clusters** (groups of competitors competing similarly)
- **White space opportunities** (underserved market positions)
- **Competitive threats** (direct head-to-head competition)
- **Differentiation opportunities** (unique positioning)

### Key Takeaways

**Maia's Unique Position:**
- **International comprehensive platform** competing against 4 US-focused competitors
- Positioned between innovation leaders (SCOIR, SchooLinks) and established players (Naviance, Xello)

**Competitive Clusters:**
1. **US Innovation Leaders** - SCOIR, SchooLinks (high innovation, US-only, growth-focused)
2. **US Established Players** - Naviance, Xello (market leaders, US-only, mature)

**Critical Findings:**
- SCOIR moving toward Maia's US private school customers (innovation threat)
- Pricing gap (Maia $10 vs. $3.50-6) creates vulnerability in US market
- All 4 competitors are US-only, leaving international market to Maia

## Methodology

### Positioning Dimensions Selected

**Map 1: Innovation Leadership vs. Market Coverage**
- **X-axis (Innovation):** AI capabilities, technology stack, feature velocity
- **Y-axis (Market Coverage):** Student reach, school count, market share
- **Purpose:** Shows innovation leaders vs. scale players

**Map 2: Pricing Strategy vs. Geographic Scope**
- **X-axis (Pricing):** Per-student cost (low to high)
- **Y-axis (Geographic Scope):** Geographic reach (single country to global)
- **Purpose:** Shows US market positioning vs. international presence

**Map 3: Feature Specialization vs. Customer Satisfaction**
- **X-axis (Specialization):** Niche focused vs. comprehensive platform
- **Y-axis (Satisfaction):** Customer review ratings
- **Purpose:** Shows specialist vs. generalist satisfaction trade-offs

### Data Sources

- Company profiles (5 platforms: Maia + 4 competitors)
- SWOT analysis (strategic assessment)
- Feature comparison matrix (50+ features across 15 categories)
- Pricing analysis (verified per-student costs)
- Customer reviews (Capterra, G2, multiple platforms)
- Technology stack comparison (AI capabilities, integrations)
- Market positioning analysis (messaging, target segments)

### Visualization Conventions

- **Platform names in CAPS** = Major competitive threat to Maia
- **Platform names in lowercase** = Limited competitive threat
- **Size of marker** = Relative market share/scale
- **Color coding:** üî¥ Very High Threat | üü° Medium-High Threat | üü¢ Low Threat

## White Space Opportunities

### Opportunity 1: US Market Innovation Leadership

**Position:** Match SCOIR AI 2.0 capabilities while maintaining comprehensive platform breadth

**Current Gap:** Maia has comprehensive features but lags SCOIR on AI innovation.

**Market Demand:**
- US private schools want cutting-edge AI (acceptance predictions, college list balancing)
- Currently choosing between: Maia (comprehensive + good service) or SCOIR (innovation + focused)
- Need platform that offers BOTH

**Addressable Via:**
- Maia develops predictive AI capabilities (Q1-Q2 2026)
- Match SCOIR AI 2.0 features: Acceptance chance predictions, AI college list balancing, AI essay review, conversational AI
- Maintain comprehensive platform advantage
- Result: "Comprehensive Innovation Leader" = defensible position

**Market Size:** Large (US private schools = primary target segment)

**Feasibility:** Medium-High
- Maia has resources (22-year company, stable revenue)
- AI development costly but achievable (license LLM vs. build from scratch)
- Timeline: 6-12 months to launch competitive AI platform

**Priority:** üî¥ URGENT - This is Maia's path to defending US market position

---

### Opportunity 2: Value Pricing Tier for US Market

**Position:** Introduce lower-priced tier to compete with SchooLinks/Xello on price

**Current Gap:** Maia $10 vs. $3.50-6 creates price barrier for budget-conscious US schools.

**Market Demand:**
- US public districts want comprehensive features but cannot afford $10/student
- Currently: Choose SchooLinks ($3.50-5.51) or Xello ($3.60) despite wanting more features
- Large underserved market (thousands of US districts price-sensitive)

**Addressable Via:**
- Maia introduces tiered pricing model:
  - **Basic Tier:** $5-6/student (core features, self-service support)
  - **Plus Tier:** $8-9/student (current feature set, standard support)
  - **Premium Tier:** $12-15/student (advanced AI, dedicated support)
- Basic tier targets value-conscious US schools
- Expand addressable US market 2-3x

**Market Size:** Very Large (10,000+ US schools currently price-blocked)

**Feasibility:** High
- Tiered pricing is operationally straightforward
- Risk: Cannibalization of existing $10 customers (mitigate via feature differentiation)

**Priority:** üü° HIGH - Significant revenue opportunity, 6-12 month initiative

---

### Opportunity 3: Native Mobile-First Platform

**Position:** Mobile-Native Experience (iOS/Android apps)

**Current Gap:** ALL 4 competitors use responsive web (no native mobile apps). Students increasingly mobile-first (smartphones primary device).

**Market Demand:**
- Students want app-based experiences (like social media: Instagram, TikTok)
- Notifications, offline access, home screen presence = higher engagement
- Xello's 20% outside-school usage shows students use CCR at home (mobile-friendly)

**Addressable Via:**
- Maia develops native iOS + Android apps (student-focused)
- Features: Portfolio, college search, AI assistant, tasks/notifications, career exploration
- Desktop web remains for counselors (counselor tools, reporting, admin)
- "Only CCR platform with native mobile app" = differentiation

**Market Size:** Large (all students, mobile-first generation)

**Feasibility:** Medium
- Mobile app development costly ($200K-500K for quality iOS+Android)
- Maintenance ongoing (OS updates, feature parity with web)
- Timeline: 9-12 months to MVP launch

**Priority:** üü° HIGH - First-mover advantage (12-18 month initiative)
