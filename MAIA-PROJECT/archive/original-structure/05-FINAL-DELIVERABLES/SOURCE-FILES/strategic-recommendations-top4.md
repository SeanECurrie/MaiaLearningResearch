## Executive Summary

### The Challenge

Maia Learning faces intensifying competition in the US market from four major platforms, each with distinct competitive advantages:
- **SCOIR:** Innovation leader (AI 2.0, Common App 2025-26), 40-50% annual growth
- **SchooLinks:** Pricing leader ($3.50-5.51 vs. Maia $10), state compliance dominance
- **Xello:** Career-first specialist (28 years, statewide contracts, 14x engagement)
- **Naviance:** Market leader (40% share) but declining (poor 3.2/5 reviews, legacy platform)

### The Opportunity

Despite competitive pressure, Maia has **defensible advantages:**
- **International moat:** 70+ countries (all 4 competitors are US-only)
- **Service excellence:** Legendary customer support vs. Naviance's poor 3.2/5 reviews
- **Comprehensive platform:** Full college + career breadth (vs. SCOIR college-focus, Xello career-focus)
- **Private school positioning:** Less price-sensitive segment (vs. SchooLinks/Xello public district focus)

### Critical Success Factors

**Three imperatives for success:**

1. **Close the Innovation Gap (6 months)** - SCOIR's AI 2.0 and Common App integration create competitive disadvantage; Maia must achieve parity by Q2 2026 or lose US private school market share

2. **Address the Pricing Challenge (12 months)** - Maia's $10 pricing is 2-3x competitors (SchooLinks $3.50-5.51, Xello $3.60); tiered pricing model expands addressable market while maintaining premium tier

3. **Defend Core Strengths** - International presence and service excellence are unique; double down on these advantages while closing gaps

### üî¥ Recommendation 2: Launch Tiered Pricing Model

**Priority:** üî¥ **CRITICAL**

**Strategic Rationale:**
Maia's single-tier $10/student pricing is 2-3x more expensive than US competitors (SchooLinks $3.50-5.51, Xello $3.60, SCOIR $5-6). This pricing gap excludes Maia from price-sensitive segments and creates competitive disadvantage. Tiered pricing expands addressable market 3-5x while maintaining premium tier for high-value customers.

**Current Pricing Challenge:**
- **Maia:** $10/student (single tier)
- **SchooLinks:** $3.50-5.51/student (50-65% less)
- **Xello:** $3.60/student (64% less)
- **SCOIR:** $5-6/student (40-50% less)
- **Naviance:** $6-8/student (20-40% less)

**Impact:** Maia cannot compete in:
- US public districts (SchooLinks/Xello pricing advantage)
- Budget-conscious US private schools (SCOIR pricing + innovation advantage)
- Value-focused segments

**Proposed Tiered Pricing Structure:**

#### Tier 1: Basic ($5-6/student)
**Target Customers:** US public districts, budget-conscious private schools, emerging markets
**Features:**
- Core college search, applications, student portfolio
- Basic AI (college matching, essay support - limited)
- Standard integrations (Common App, Naviance import)
- Email support (48-hour response)

**Competitive Positioning:** Matches SCOIR ($5-6), competes with SchooLinks ($3.50-5.51)

#### Tier 2: Plus ($8-9/student)
**Target Customers:** Current Maia customer profile (US private schools, international schools)
**Features:**
- ALL Basic features PLUS:
- Advanced AI (acceptance predictions, list balancing, conversational assistant)
- Enhanced career exploration
- Scholarships and financial aid tools
- Work-based learning tracking
- Priority email support (24-hour response)

**Competitive Positioning:** Between SCOIR ($5-6) and current Maia ($10); value positioning

#### Tier 3: Premium ($12-15/student)
**Target Customers:** High-touch customers, large districts, international school networks, elite private schools
**Features:**
- ALL Plus features PLUS:
- Dedicated account manager
- White-glove onboarding and migration support
- Custom integrations and reporting
- Strategic consulting (college counseling best practices)
- Phone + email support (4-hour response)
- Quarterly business reviews

**Competitive Positioning:** Premium justified by international presence, service excellence, comprehensive platform

**Implementation Plan:**

**Phase 1 (Q1 2026): Design**
- Feature allocation (which features in which tier)
- Pricing research (willingness to pay, competitive benchmarking)
- Internal financial modeling (costs, margins, revenue projections)

**Phase 2 (Q2 2026): Build**
- Technical infrastructure (feature flagging, access controls)
- Billing system updates (multiple tiers, upgrades/downgrades)
- Sales training (how to sell each tier)
- Marketing materials (tier comparison guides)

**Phase 3 (Q3 2026): Beta Launch**
- Beta test with 10-20 schools per tier
- Gather feedback (pricing sensitivity, feature value)
- Optimize (adjust pricing/features based on data)

**Phase 4 (Q4 2026): Full Launch**
- Public announcement (conferences, press release)
- Migrate existing customers (grandfathered at $10, option to upgrade to Premium or downgrade to Basic/Plus)
- Open sales to new segments (US public districts with Basic tier)

**Revenue Impact Scenarios:**

| Scenario | Volume Change | Price Change | Net Revenue Impact |
|----------|---------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Conservative | +30% | -10% avg | +17% revenue |
| Moderate | +50% | -5% avg | +42% revenue |
| Aggressive | +100% | -15% avg | +70% revenue |

**Risk Mitigation:**
- **Cannibalization:** Existing $10 customers downgrade to $5-6 Basic
- **Mitigation:** Feature differentiation (Basic has limited AI, no career, no dedicated support) ensures Plus/Premium value clear
- **Grandfathering:** Existing customers keep $10 rate, can upgrade to Premium ($12-15) or downgrade to Basic/Plus if desired

**Success Metrics:**
- Tiered pricing launched by Q4 2026
- Addressable market expands 3-5x (can now compete in value segments)
- Volume increase exceeds price decrease (net revenue positive)
- Customer satisfaction maintained (no backlash from existing customers)

**Investment:** $150-250K (product development, billing system, sales/marketing materials)

**Addresses Competitors:** üî¥ **SchooLinks** (pricing leader $3.50-5.51), üü° **Xello** (pricing $3.60)

**Urgency:** üî¥ **HIGH** - Pricing gap is immediate barrier to market expansion; address within 12 months
