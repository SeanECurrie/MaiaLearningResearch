### Quick Overview

Common Application is a **nonprofit membership organization** that provides a standardized college application platform, allowing students to apply to multiple colleges (1,000+) using a single application. Founded in 1975 by 15 private liberal arts colleges, Common App has become the **dominant college application infrastructure** in the United States, processing applications from nearly 1.5 million students annually.

**Common App is NOT a competitor to platforms like Maia Learning, Naviance, or SCOIR.** Instead, it is a **critical ecosystem partner** that these platforms integrate with to enable their students to submit college applications. Understanding Common App is essential because:
1. It is the **required application method** for students applying to most US colleges
2. **Integration with Common App** is a competitive necessity for college counseling platforms
3. **SCOIR's NEW 2025-26 Common App integration** is a major competitive advantage
4. Common App's policies, features, and partnerships directly impact all competitors

**Key Characteristics:**
- **50-year history** (founded 1975, now celebrating golden anniversary)
- **1,100+ member colleges** (up from 15 founding members)
- **1.5M applicants** in 2024-25 season (5% growth year-over-year)

- **Direct Admissions program:** 200+ colleges offering proactive admissions (2025-26)
- **Integration platform:** Enables third-party software (Naviance, SCOIR, Maia, others) to connect

**Strategic Position:**
Common App occupies a **unique position as essential infrastructure** in the US college admissions ecosystem. It is not in competition with Maia Learning or similar platforms; rather, these platforms depend on Common App integration to deliver value to their students. The organization's nonprofit status, mission-driven approach, and industry-standard position make it a **permanent fixture** of the college admissions landscape.

## Platform Overview

### Common Application System

**Core Functionality:**

**1. Single Application, Multiple Colleges:**
- Students fill out ONE application
- Can apply to 20+ colleges with same core application
- Saves time, reduces repetition
- Centralizes application management

**2. Application Components:**

**Profile Section:**
- Personal information (name, address, demographics)
- Family information (parents, siblings)
- Education history (schools attended)
- Testing (SAT, ACT scores - optional for many colleges)
- Activities (up to 10 extracurricular activities)
- Honors and awards

**Writing Section:**
- **Common App Essay:** One personal statement (650 words max)
- **Essay Prompts:** 7 prompts to choose from (updated annually)
- **Supplemental Essays:** College-specific additional essays

**Courses & Grades:**
- High school coursework listing
- Self-reported grades (verified by counselor)
- Course rigor indicators

**Recommender and FERPA Section:**
- Teacher recommendation requests (typically 2)
- Counselor recommendation (1)
- Additional recommenders (optional)
- FERPA waiver (privacy rights)

**3. Recommendation System:**
- **Integrated workflow** for teachers and counselors
- **Common App Recommender** portal
- **Integration with school platforms:** Naviance, SCOIR, Maia Learning, others
- **Submission tracking:** Students can monitor recommendation status

**4. College-Specific Sections:**
- **Supplemental questions:** Each college adds its own questions
- **Program-specific requirements:** Major selection, portfolios, etc.
- **Institutional supplements:** Additional essays, short answers

### Direct Admissions Program (NEW)

**Overview:**
Common App's **Direct Admissions** program is a revolutionary new feature launched at scale in 2024-25, offering **proactive admission offers** to qualified students before they even apply.

**How It Works:**

**For Students:**
1. Student creates Common App account
2. Enters basic profile information (GPA, courses, demographics)
3. **Common App automatically identifies** students eligible for Direct Admissions
4. **Admission offers appear** directly in the application dashboard
5. Students can **accept by applying** (simplified process, often no essays required)

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- **First-generation students** (neither parent has 4-year degree)
- **Low- and middle-income students** (fee waiver eligible or near-eligible)
- **Minimum GPA requirements:** Varies by college (typically 2.5-3.0)
- **No test scores required:** Based on GPA alone

**Participating Colleges (2025-26):**
- **200+ colleges and universities**
- Mix of public and private institutions
- Range of selectivity levels (safety to match schools)
- **Growth trajectory:** 116 colleges (2024-25) → 200+ colleges (2025-26)

**Impact:**
- **Removes application barriers:** No essays, fees, or stress
- **Increases college-going rates:** Students who might not have applied otherwise
- **Equity advancement:** Targets underrepresented populations
- **Non-binding offers:** Students can still apply elsewhere, decline offer

**Strategic Significance:**
Direct Admissions represents a **potential disruption** to the traditional college counseling model. If students receive admission offers automatically, the value proposition of counseling platforms may shift from "help students apply" to "help students choose wisely among offers."
