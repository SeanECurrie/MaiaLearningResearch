# Enterprise Consulting Frameworks

This reference provides proven frameworks used by top-tier consulting firms for company research and competitive analysis.

## Strategic Analysis Frameworks

### Porter's Five Forces
Analyze competitive intensity and attractiveness of an industry:

1. **Threat of New Entrants** - Barriers to entry, capital requirements, economies of scale
2. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers** - Supplier concentration, switching costs, uniqueness
3. **Bargaining Power of Buyers** - Buyer concentration, price sensitivity, switching costs
4. **Threat of Substitutes** - Alternative solutions, price-performance trade-off
5. **Competitive Rivalry** - Number of competitors, industry growth, differentiation

### SWOT Analysis
Internal and external strategic positioning:

- **Strengths** - Internal competitive advantages, core competencies, unique resources
- **Weaknesses** - Internal limitations, resource gaps, competitive disadvantages
- **Opportunities** - External market trends, unmet needs, emerging technologies
- **Threats** - External competitive forces, regulatory changes, market shifts

### Value Chain Analysis
Examine activities that create competitive advantage:

**Primary Activities:**
- Inbound logistics, Operations, Outbound logistics, Marketing & Sales, Service

**Support Activities:**
- Firm infrastructure, HR management, Technology development, Procurement

### Business Model Canvas
Map the business model across nine elements:

1. Customer Segments
2. Value Propositions
3. Channels
4. Customer Relationships
5. Revenue Streams
6. Key Resources
7. Key Activities
8. Key Partnerships
9. Cost Structure

## Competitive Analysis Frameworks

### Strategic Group Mapping
Plot competitors on key dimensions (e.g., price vs. quality, breadth vs. depth) to identify competitive groups and strategic positioning.

### Competitive Positioning Matrix
Compare competitors across critical success factors:
- Create weighted scoring across dimensions
- Identify market leaders, challengers, followers, and nichers

### Market Share Analysis
Analyze relative market positions:
- Absolute market share
- Relative market share (vs. largest competitor)
- Market concentration (HHI index)

## Market Research Frameworks

### TAM/SAM/SOM Analysis
Size the market opportunity:

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** - Total market demand
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market)** - Segment target can serve
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** - Realistic capture

### Customer Segmentation
Segment analysis approaches:

- **Demographic** - Age, income, location, company size
- **Psychographic** - Values, lifestyle, attitudes
- **Behavioral** - Usage patterns, loyalty, benefits sought
- **Firmographic** (B2B) - Industry, revenue, employee count

### Jobs-to-be-Done Framework
Understand customer needs:
- Functional jobs (tasks to accomplish)
- Social jobs (how they want to be perceived)
- Emotional jobs (how they want to feel)

## Financial Analysis Frameworks

### DuPont Analysis
Decompose ROE into component drivers:
- ROE = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier

### Unit Economics
Analyze per-customer profitability:
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Lifetime Value (LTV)
- LTV:CAC ratio
- Payback period

### Growth Metrics
Key indicators for growth companies:
- Revenue growth rate (YoY, MoM)
- Customer growth rate
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)

## Go-to-Market Analysis

### Distribution Channel Analysis
Evaluate channel strategies:
- Direct vs. indirect
- Channel economics (margins, costs)
- Channel conflict
- Omnichannel integration

### Pricing Strategy Analysis
Compare pricing approaches:
- Cost-plus vs. value-based
- Freemium models
- Subscription vs. transaction
- Price discrimination strategies

### Sales Model Comparison
Analyze sales approaches:
- Inside sales vs. field sales
- Self-service vs. high-touch
- Sales cycle length
- Average deal size

## Product/Technology Analysis

### Feature Comparison Matrix
Systematically compare product capabilities:
- Must-have vs. nice-to-have features
- Feature parity analysis
- Innovation differentiation

### Technology Stack Assessment
Evaluate technical capabilities:
- Core technologies used
- Integration capabilities
- Scalability architecture
- Security and compliance

### Product Roadmap Analysis
Assess strategic direction:
- Announced features
- Patent filings
- Acquisitions
- Technology partnerships
