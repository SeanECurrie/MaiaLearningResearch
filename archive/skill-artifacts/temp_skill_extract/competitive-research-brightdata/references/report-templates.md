# Report Templates and Structures

This reference provides proven report structures for different types of competitive analysis and company research deliverables.

## Company Profile Report

### Executive Summary (1 page)
- Company overview (1-2 sentences)
- Key findings (3-5 bullet points)
- Strategic implications (2-3 sentences)

### Company Overview
- **Founded:** Year, location, founders
- **Headquarters:** Current location(s)
- **Size:** Employees, revenue (if public)
- **Ownership:** Public/private, investors, parent company
- **Mission/Vision:** Company purpose and direction

### Business Model
- **Products/Services:** Core offerings and categories
- **Target Market:** Customer segments served
- **Revenue Model:** How they make money
- **Distribution:** How they reach customers
- **Key Differentiators:** Competitive advantages

### Market Position
- **Market Share:** Position in primary markets
- **Competitive Positioning:** How they differentiate
- **Growth Trajectory:** Recent growth and momentum

### Strategic Initiatives
- Recent product launches
- Market expansion efforts
- Partnerships and alliances
- M&A activity

### Financial Highlights (if available)
- Revenue and growth
- Profitability
- Funding/valuation
- Key metrics

### Strengths and Weaknesses
- **Strengths:** 3-5 key advantages
- **Weaknesses:** 3-5 key challenges

### Outlook and Implications
- Future direction
- Threats and opportunities
- Strategic recommendations

## Competitive Analysis Report

### Executive Summary
- Analysis scope and methodology
- Key findings
- Strategic recommendations
- Critical decision points

### Market Landscape
- **Market Size and Growth:** TAM/SAM/SOM
- **Key Trends:** Major shifts affecting the market
- **Regulatory Environment:** Relevant regulations
- **Technology Disruptions:** Emerging technologies

### Competitive Landscape Overview
- **Number of Players:** Market fragmentation
- **Market Leaders:** Top 3-5 players and share
- **Competitive Intensity:** High/Medium/Low with rationale
- **Barriers to Entry:** What protects incumbents

### Detailed Competitor Profiles
For each major competitor:

**Company X**
- Background and overview
- Product/service portfolio
- Target segments
- Business model
- Pricing strategy
- Go-to-market approach
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Recent developments

### Head-to-Head Comparison
Create comparison tables across:
- Product features and capabilities
- Pricing and packaging
- Target customers
- Geographic coverage
- Technology stack
- Customer satisfaction
- Market share

### Competitive Positioning
- Strategic group map
- Positioning matrix
- White space opportunities

### Strategic Implications
- Competitive threats assessment
- Opportunities to exploit
- Recommended strategic responses
- Investment priorities

### Appendix
- Methodology and sources
- Additional data tables
- Detailed financial comparisons

## Market Entry Analysis Report

### Executive Summary
- Market opportunity assessment
- Entry barriers and risks
- Recommended entry strategy
- Expected ROI and timeline

### Market Opportunity
- **Market Size:** Current and projected
- **Growth Drivers:** Why market is attractive
- **Customer Needs:** Jobs to be done
- **Willingness to Pay:** Price sensitivity analysis

### Competitive Dynamics
- Current competitive landscape
- Expected competitive response
- Sustainable differentiation opportunities

### Entry Barriers Assessment
- **Capital Requirements:** Upfront investment needed
- **Regulatory Hurdles:** Licensing, compliance
- **Technology Requirements:** Technical capabilities needed
- **Distribution Access:** Channel availability
- **Brand Recognition:** Need for brand building
- **Economies of Scale:** Minimum efficient scale

### Entry Strategy Options
Compare 3-4 entry approaches:

**Option 1: [e.g., Organic Build]**
- Description and approach
- Pros and cons
- Required investment
- Timeline to market
- Risk level

**Option 2: [e.g., Partnership/Alliance]**
- Description and approach
- Pros and cons
- Required investment
- Timeline to market
- Risk level

### Financial Projections
- Investment requirements (Year 0-3)
- Revenue projections (Year 1-5)
- Break-even analysis
- ROI calculation
- Sensitivity analysis

### Risk Assessment
- Market risks
- Competitive risks
- Execution risks
- Mitigation strategies

### Recommended Strategy
- Preferred entry mode
- Phased implementation plan
- Success metrics and KPIs
- Go/no-go decision criteria

## Product Comparison Report

### Executive Summary
- Products being compared
- Key findings
- Winner by category
- Recommendation

### Comparison Scope
- Products/versions compared
- Evaluation criteria
- Methodology and sources

### Feature-by-Feature Comparison

**Category: [e.g., Core Functionality]**

| Feature | Product A | Product B | Product C | Winner |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Feature 1 | Details | Details | Details | Product X |
| Feature 2 | Details | Details | Details | Product Y |

Repeat for each category:
- Core functionality
- User experience
- Integration capabilities
- Security and compliance
- Scalability and performance
- Support and documentation

### Pricing Comparison
- Pricing models
- Total cost of ownership (TCO)
- Value for money analysis

### Use Case Analysis
Evaluate fitness for different scenarios:

**Use Case 1: [e.g., Enterprise Deployment]**
- Requirements
- Best fit product and why
- Trade-offs

### Qualitative Assessment
- **Ease of Implementation:** Setup and deployment
- **User Experience:** Interface and usability
- **Vendor Stability:** Company health and trajectory
- **Customer Support:** Quality and responsiveness
- **Community and Ecosystem:** Third-party resources

### Strengths and Weaknesses

**Product A**
- Strengths (3-5 points)
- Weaknesses (3-5 points)

**Product B**
- Strengths (3-5 points)
- Weaknesses (3-5 points)

### Recommendation Matrix
Recommend best product for different buyer profiles:

| Buyer Profile | Recommended Product | Rationale |
|---------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Small business | Product A | Why it fits |
| Mid-market | Product B | Why it fits |
| Enterprise | Product C | Why it fits |

## Presentation Deck Structure

For client-facing presentations:

**Slide 1:** Title and Executive Summary
**Slide 2:** Agenda and Methodology
**Slide 3:** Market Overview
**Slide 4:** Competitive Landscape
**Slides 5-N:** Competitor Profiles (1 slide each)
**Slide N+1:** Competitive Positioning Map
**Slide N+2:** Feature Comparison Matrix
**Slide N+3:** Strengths/Weaknesses Summary
**Slide N+4:** Strategic Implications
**Slide N+5:** Recommendations
**Slide N+6:** Next Steps

Keep slides visual with:
- Maximum 5-7 bullets per slide
- Key metrics highlighted
- Charts and graphs for data
- Clear takeaways
