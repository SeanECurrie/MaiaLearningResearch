## Skill Capabilities

### 1. Fact-Checking

**Verifies:**
- Company information (founded dates, CEO names, headquarters)
- Market data (market share, customer counts, geographic presence)
- Product features (availability, integrations, launch dates)
- Pricing claims (costs, comparisons, transparency)
- Customer reviews (ratings, testimonial accuracy)
- News items (acquisitions, funding, recent developments)

**Methods:**
- Web search to verify claims
- Bright Data tools for deeper verification
- Multiple source triangulation (2-3 independent sources)
- Primary source prioritization (company sites, official reports)
- Cross-referencing with original research sources

### 2. Source Validation

**Checks:**
- URL accessibility (no broken links)
- Content accuracy (source actually says what's claimed)
- Date currency (information recent and relevant)
- Author credibility (source authoritative)
- Context accuracy (quotes not out of context)

**Documents:**
- ‚úÖ Verified: Source confirms claim
- ‚ö†Ô∏è Partially Verified: Source supports with caveats
- ‚ùå Not Verified: Source doesn't support claim
- üîó Broken: URL inaccessible or content changed
- üìÖ Outdated: Source too old or superseded

### 3. Consistency Analysis

**Reviews:**
- Internal consistency (within documents)
- Cross-document consistency (across profiles, analyses)
- Numerical consistency (calculations correct, numbers align)
- Timeline verification (dates chronological and accurate)
- Terminology consistency (same concepts called same things)

### 4. Gap Identification

**Identifies:**
- Sparse citations (major claims with only 1-2 sources)
- Missing sources (assertions without evidence)
- Outdated information (old sources when recent available)
- Conflicting information (unresolved contradictions)
- Missing comparisons (incomplete competitive analysis)

### 5. Quality Assessment

**Evaluates Against:**
- McKinsey/BCG/Bain quality standards
- Consulting industry benchmarks
- Academic research standards
- Industry analyst report standards (Gartner, Forrester)

**Scores:**
- Evidence quality
- Analysis quality  
- Internal consistency
- Writing & presentation
- Overall quality score (0-100%)

## Quality Standards Reference

### What Makes "Consulting-Grade" Quality?

**Evidence:**
- All major claims backed by 2-3 independent sources
- Primary sources prioritized (company sites, official data)
- Information recent (within 12 months for current data)
- Conflicts noted and explained
- Data gaps explicitly acknowledged

**Analysis:**
- Frameworks applied correctly
- Logical flow from evidence to conclusions
- Alternative explanations considered
- Limitations stated
- Recommendations specific and actionable

**Presentation:**
- Executive summary captures key findings (1-2 pages)
- Clear structure with logical flow
- Professional writing (concise, active voice)
- Data visualizations accurate and insightful
- Suitable for C-level presentation

**Quality Score Interpretation:**
- 95-100%: Outstanding, publication-grade
- 85-94%: Very good, client-ready with minor polish
- 75-84%: Good, generally client-ready
- 60-74%: Acceptable, improvements needed before delivery
- Below 60%: Needs major work, not client-ready

## Efficiency Tips

### Batch Similar Verifications
Instead of verifying claims one-by-one, group similar types:
- All founding dates together
- All market share claims together
- All pricing data together

This is more efficient because:
- Less tool switching
- Consistent verification method
- Easier to spot patterns
- Can use batch search operations

### Prioritize Critical Claims
Focus verification effort on:
1. Executive summary claims (highest visibility)
2. Strategic recommendations (highest impact)
3. Comparative claims (most scrutinized)
4. Recent news (most likely to be outdated)

Less critical (if time limited):
- Historical background
- Company founding stories
- Detailed feature descriptions

### Use Appropriate Review Depth
- **Quick Validation:** Before meetings, time-constrained
- **Standard Review:** Before client delivery, important internal
- **Comprehensive Audit:** High-stakes, publication, formal audits
- **Targeted:** Specific concerns or disputes

Don't over-invest in QA when quick validation sufficient.
